Experience Design

Strategy & Vision

Large ships do not turn quickly. That was the metaphor of how I approached implementing Verizon Wireless's User Experience Design program in 2006.

Defining the Vision Blueprint
The Verizon Mobile Lifestyle

As the Director of User Experience Design for Verizon's Consumer Product and Services Organization, it was my responsibility to define a vision for where I would take the Customer and User Experience of the organizations's $14B portfolio of mass-consumer offerings. This vision was provided to C-level management and executed at the corporate level - ensuring Verizon would maintain market leadership and drive the crucial evolution of the mobile eco-system customers know today.

Improving the Out-of-Box Experience

Creating great experiences for products goes well beyond traditional software interfaces. Perceptions of a product are created at every touchpoint and subtle details matter. Design patterns and strategies extend beyond user interfaces.

Jetpack Packaging Prototype

This video captures assessing a new product, out-of-box, unpacking experience, for a Verizon Jetpack in development. My "single-hand" approach, was used to access and improve the first-run experience - keeping overall accessibility and ease-of-use in mind. The ultimate goal of this test was to set up and use the product with a single hand.

Improving Collateral for Certified Like New Devices (CLNR)

This illustration was created to provide clarity, visually reinforce process, and eliminate user frustation around cumbersome SIM card removal from certain dedicated mobile hotspots. The illustration was included on printed collateral adjacent to user friendly instructions and was provided with specific certified like new replacement Jetpacks. Prior to production use, the collateral sheet was tested for clarity and representation accuracy.
