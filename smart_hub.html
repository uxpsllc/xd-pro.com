Verizon Smart Hub

Project Summary

Role
  • Data Devices Portfolio Experience Design Manager
  • Lead Sr. Experience Designer
  • UX Researcher

Ambition & Scope

Build a next-generation wireless home router with voice and an integrated home controls platform accessible via a companion mobile app with IFTTT capability.


Result

Product commericalization with recurring revenue stream.

New Product Concept Sketch

A new hardware product design language was being introduced for Verizon branded products. Part of evolving the product portfolio also included expanding functionality and capability of peripherial products in the the consumer market.

Concept Evolution For Requirements Definition

I produced concept illustrations of ideal user expereince aspects surrounding the integration of a display along with other technical capabilities.

Requirements Definition

Initial marketing requirements were drafted to identify the target segment, specific users, product use cases, and other items which addressed the business case and rationale for the project.

Working in a textually abstract way allowed for sharing of ideas with other stakeholders. Collectively, the cross-functional product team contributed to the definition process. Cross-functional stakeholders collaboratively weighed in with domain expertise.

New Product Requirements Formation - Users & Use Cases

I reviewed an contributed to the definition and evolution of the early product requirements in support the Product Line Manager that would ultimately be responsible for the entire Go-to-market plan, P&L, and product lifecyle.

Device Experience Design Requirements Formation

Starting with the base set of requirements for all Data devices in the portfolio, I began to draft experience requirements for the new product.

Experience Flows & Early Service Blueprinting



The out-of-box and first-run experience was defined through multiple iterations of experience flows and service blueprinting of system and network touchpoints that would impacts on the user experience.

Here, additional requirements were developed and the beginning of error hanlding was also being documented.

First Run Flow



Product first run experience evolved and increased in fidelity through multiple iterations and testing sessions to verify design decisions.

Various branches of the first-run UX were also fleshed out.
