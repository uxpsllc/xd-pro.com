Summary

I led a cross-functional onboarding inquiry to map realities across regions, define success metrics, and design patterns that shorten time-to-access and improve early-tenure confidence. The work produced a unified journey framework, provisioning flows, and a prioritized roadmap grounded in evidence.

Note: Excerpts shown here are intentionally reduced to protect IP.

Context & Challenge

New hires often lose days waiting for system access, role clarity, equipment, and introductions. Ownership is split across HR, IT, and business operations, creating gaps that erode early momentum. We needed one coherent experience—from pre-boarding to Day-90—with clear handoffs and measurable outcomes.

Approach

Discover & Align

I ran design-thinking workshops in Miro to capture business and technical requirements, user needs, and priority use cases; then closed gaps through contextual inquiry with regional SMEs to understand local workflows and policy constraints.

Design & Prototype

Using the verified requirements, I produced service blueprints and UX flows—plus a Figma-based component system for provisioning, access requests, and Day-1 setup—so we could quickly test and iterate.

Build & Govern

I formalized version-controlled requirements (functional/non-functional, accessibility) as the single source of truth for hand-off; seeded UAT scenarios; and defined KPIs (time-to-access, completion rates, first-week satisfaction) to track impact post-launch.

Test & Iterate

We validated with representative roles and regions, using findings to refine content, sequencing, and escalation paths; insights informed the implementation roadmap and localization guidance.

Outcomes

  • Unified journey & taxonomy aligning HR, IT, and business handoffs.
  • Provisioning patterns & flows ready for pilot and scale.
  • Measurement plan to track time-to-access, role clarity, and early-tenure satisfaction.
  • Prioritized roadmap sequencing quick wins and foundational investments.
