Concrete
  • Investigate & Understand

    Creating innovative and desirable solutions requires identifying needs and opportunities. My process starts with discovery - conducting research to gain insights about an area of interest. I employ a methodology of digging into the problem space to thoroughly understand the landscape of what I am attempting to solve and who I am solving it for.

  • Define & Design

    I believe design itself is a process and good design takes time. But great designs, they evolve. Using data-insights derived from the initial investigative process grounds my preliminary design approach. For green-field areas, employing participatory techniques with targeted customers or users opens up avenues for innovation to occur. Testing early and often is crucial for success.


    More »

  • Test & Verify

    Ensuring requirements and design strategies are right, requires gathering feedback throughout development. Putting concept designs in front of humans that will interact with the end solution and using their input to actively verify the design descisions are on track, improves all aspects of solution development. Participant feedback also allows for discovery and reduction of costly usability issues.
